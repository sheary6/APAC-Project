Information and Communications Technology

The Public Service Capability Enhancement Project (PSCEP) is an initiative designed to improve the overall effectiveness, efficiency, and responsiveness of public services in India. The project aims to strengthen government institutions, enhance the skills of public service personnel, and ensure that the public sector can deliver high-quality services to citizens, contributing to national development.

Funded by the Government of India

Renewable Energy

The Innovation in Solar Power and Hybrid Technologies Project is an initiative by the Government of India, supported by the World Bank, aimed at demonstrating the operational and economic feasibility of utility-scale innovative renewable energy technologies and battery energy storage solutions. The project also seeks to strengthen institutional capacity to facilitate the commercial scale-up of such technologies in India.

Funded by the Government of India

Urban, Resilience, and Land

The Green National Highways Corridor Project (GNHCP) is an infrastructure development initiative aimed at building environmentally sustainable highways across India. The project integrates green and climate-resilient technologies into highway construction and maintenance, ensuring a balance between infrastructure growth and environmental conservation.

Funded by the Government of India

Financial and Banking Service

India SEWA 2025 was a digital financial inclusion initiative aimed at empowering informal sector workers, particularly self-employed women, small- scale entrepreneurs, and daily wage earners. The project focused on providing access to digital financial services, enhancing financial literacy, and enabling economic independence through technology-driven solutions.

Self-employed women

Education

The Strengthening Teaching-Learning and Results for States (STARS) Project aims to improve the quality of education across India by enhancing teaching practices, student outcomes, and the overall education system. The project focuses on improving curriculum, teacher training, learning assessments, and state-level governance to ensure effective implementation of reforms and bridge existing gaps in education. It emphasizes results-based improvements and aims to address challenges in the educational system at the grassroots level.

Strengthen Learning Outcomes

Water and Wastewater

The Punjab Municipal Services Improvement Project (PMSIP) is a collaborative initiative between the Government of Punjab, India, and the World Bank, aimed at enhancing urban governance, financial management, and the delivery of sustainable water services in the cities of Amritsar and Ludhiana. Approved in March 2021, the project seeks to address critical urban infrastructure challenges and improve the quality of municipal services.
